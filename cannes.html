Cannes Lions: Shaping digital experiences for the world’s top creativity festivals

2016 — 2020

CHALLENGE

The Cannes Lions digital estate was a collection of disconnected tools, each built in isolation with its own UX debt. For delegates navigating a five-day global event, constant context-switching produced a digital experience that fundamentally undermined the prestige of the brand.

STRATEGY

I led product design across a full-scale platform unification — architecting a single system serving four distinct user archetypes: Delegates, Agencies, Jurors, and Partners. Built on a white-label foundation, the same architecture was designed to power sister festivals across Dubai, Singapore, and Europe with minimal rework.

RESULTS

Reduced checkout abandonment by 18%, accelerated corporate transaction closure by 35%, and cut the design-to-development cycle by 40% through a unified Design System — now powering 5 global festival brands.

CHALLENGE

A System Under Pressure

Between 2017 and 2020, the Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity underwent a full-scale digital transformation. As Product Design Lead, I guided this evolution—redesigning the platform that powers the world’s most prestigious celebration of creativity. The digital estate was a collection of disconnected tools. Each was built in isolation, carrying its own logic and UX debt. For delegates navigating a five-day global event, this meant constant context-switching and a digital experience that fundamentally undermined the prestige of the brand.

STRATEGY

Systems Thinking Throughout

The brief was structural rather than cosmetic. I needed to architect a unified platform capable of serving four distinct user archetypes: Delegates, Agencies, Jurors, and Partners. This system also had to provide a white-label foundation for sister festivals across Dubai, Singapore, and Europe.

I anchored the process in a Double Diamond framework, but the real discipline was resisting the urge to jump to visual solutions. We ran friction analyses across live festival cycles, audited manual staff workflows, and mapped behavioral data to identify exactly where the system was failing.

The core insight was that the platform was not failing because of aesthetics. It was failing because it lacked a coherent information model. Every downstream issue, from abandoned checkouts to juror fatigue, traced back to the same root cause of fragmentation.

The Intelligence of Discovery

The festival programme is the most used and was previously the most broken part of the platform. Three soloed verticals (Explore, Programme, and Speakers) forced users to reconcile information across disparate interfaces.

We collapsed these into a single experience and introduced a Timeline Layer. This visual conflict-resolution tool allowed delegates to see session overlaps in real time and make deliberate trade-offs. Combined with a robust Save and Sync feature, the transition from desktop planning to on-site mobile execution became seamless.

E-Commerce: Designing for High-Stakes B2B

Ticketing at this scale is an account management problem sitting inside a consumer interface. Agencies represent 60% of revenue and require bulk purchasing, attendee rostering, and ancillary service layering for hotels and VIP access.

Rather than compromise the UI, we designed distinct flows for each archetype, unified by a shared underlying component system. This led to a 35% acceleration in corporate transaction closure and an 18% reduction in abandonment. This same architecture was later deployed across Dubai Lynx, Spikes Asia, and Eurobest with minimal rework.

Judging: Protecting Cognitive Bandwidth

Jurors face a specific kind of cognitive depletion that poor UI accelerates. The legacy interface forced constant context-switching between categories, destroying momentum.

I redesigned the dashboard as a "Single Pane of Glass" featuring clear prioritization and inline scoring. Jurors completed reviews 25% faster. We did not rush the experience; we simply removed every obstacle between the judge and the creative work.

RESULTS

A New Global Baseline

What began as a redesign of a single product evolved into a resilient digital foundation for the entire Lions brand family. The architecture was robust enough to be partially integrated by Money 20/20, a fintech brand in an entirely different vertical. Beyond the metrics, the digital experience finally reflects the prestige of the event it serves.
